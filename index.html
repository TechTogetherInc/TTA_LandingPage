About TechTogether

TechTogether works to elevate the access, exposure, and inclusion of gender-marginalized individuals in interdisciplinary fields of technology. We do this by hosting events like hackathons, promoting inclusivity-focused research, and providing professional development resources.

TechTogether was founded in 2017 as SheHacks by 13 college students across Boston. What was once a one-time event is now a national organization that serves individuals around the world.

About TechTogether Atlanta

TechTogether Atlanta is Atlanta's hackathon for gender-marginalized individuals. With a team from all over the world, Tech Together Atlanta is striving to provide support and opportunities virtually to gender-marginalized hackers.

Challenges

Best Self Care Hack

This prize is for the team who builds a hack focused on self love, mental health, and general wellness.

Each winning team member will receive an yearly subscription of Calm or HeadSpace.

Best Social Good Hack

This prize if for mission driven hacks to make the world a better place.

Each winning team member will receive a 32 oz. Wide Mouth HydroFlask bottle.

Best Interactive Art Hack

This prize is for the hack that integrates art, user interaction and technology.

Each winning team member will receive a Polaroid and Instax Film.

Best Work From Home Hack

This prize is for the hack made for students, employees and anyone working from home.

Each winning team member will receive a RocketBook Reusable Notebook.

Most Courageous Hack

This prize is for teams with 3 or more first-time hackers.

Each winning team member will receive a $10 Grubhub gift card.

The Prettiest Hack

This prize is for the hack with the best design and UI/UX.

Each winning team member will receive a $10 Amazon gift card.

The Ultimate Failed Hack

This prize is for the team who dreamed big and failed hard.

Each winning team member will receive a $10 Amazon gift card for trying to make the ultimate hack.

Best High School Hack by BU Spark

The best hack created by a team consisting of all high school students.

Each winning team member will receive a $20 Amazon gift card.

Best Financial Hack by Capital One

Create a fun financial hack!

Each winning team member will receive a $200 Amazon gift card.

Best AR/VR Hack by echoAR

Build the best AR/VR application using the echoAR platform! The only rule is that you must use the echoAR platform.

Each team will receive a $50 Amazon Gift Card and each team member will receive a 1 month-free Business Tier access to echoAR.

Best “Retail, Banking, or Restaurant” Hack by NCR

Develop a disruptive and innovative solution that solves at least one of NCR’s most significant problems for Banks, Stores, or Restaurants.

Each winning team member will receive a $100 gift card from one of the following: Amazon, UberEats, or Visa.

Gamification for Education by Microsoft

Gamification is defined as “the application of game-design elements and game principles in non-game contexts”. So, how are you and your team going to help people learn in a fun way?

Each member will receive: Xbox Game Pass Ultimate & LinkedIn Learning Membership. As a team: $1,000 USD to be donated to an approved charity of their choice to support our joint initiative – social good!

FAQ

Where do I submit my project?
Your project will be submitted through Devpost. Click on the "Submit Project" button at the top of the site to access the Devpost. Your submission must include a video (no longer than 2 minutes) that showcases your project, a basic description of your project, and the challenge(s) for which you want your project to be considered.
Where do I go to attend a session?
TechTogether Atlanta workshops, activities and events will be taking place via Zoom, so make sure you check the event schedule and Discord announcements for the latest updates on how to get in.
How to I find a mentor for help?
Mentoring will all be done through Discord. To request a mentor, fill out the form on the Discord channel and a mentor will be in touch with you as soon as they are available!
I did not get an invite to join discord. How do I get the invite?
To receive an invitation to the TechTogether Atlanta server, register via Eventbrite and fill out our check-in form. Once everything is filled out, we'll be in touch and get you the access you need.
What is the project submission deadline?
Make sure to check the event schedule to stay up to date on important hackathon deadlines and everything you need to know about what's going on throughout the weekend! Announcements and reminders will also be broadcasted on the Discord server in case you forget.
Where can I access workshop recordings?
Workshop recordings will be available via a Google Drive folder and shared with participants following the completion of the event. So don't worry if you happen to miss one of this weekend's workshops because we got you covered!
What if I don’t have a team? Can I work alone?
All teams must be between 2-6 people. While you have the option to work alone, if you want to submit your project you must work with at least one other person. Don't worry if you don't have a team—you'll have plenty of opportunities to find one virtually! TechTogether will be hosting a variety of team formation activities throughout the first day, and we will also have a Discord channel dedicated to team formation leading up to and during the event.
Who is eligible to participate?
This hackathon is for women (cis & trans) and non-binary partcipants only. Men are welcome to mentor, volunteer, judge, or participate as sponsors! You must be 14 years or older to participate in this event. There are no location restrictions!
How many people can be on a team?
All teams must be between 2-6 people. We highly encourage that advanced hackers bring on beginner hackers to mentor throughout the event.
